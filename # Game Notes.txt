# Tactical Prototype – Gameplay Notes (Current Build)

## Core Gameplay Structure

The prototype is a grid-based tactical RPG inspired by Fire Emblem, Advance Wars, and formation-focused strategy games.

The current focus is:

* movement + positioning
* directional defense / coverage
* stamina-based reactions
* ranged line-of-sight combat
* terrain interaction
* controlled retreat gameplay

The project currently uses:

* square grid movement
* directional facing
* defensive coverage zones
* delayed action confirmation
* stamina-based counter systems

# Stamina System

## Purpose

Stamina limits reaction attacks after movement.

This creates:

* real formation exhaustion
* vulnerable advances
* stronger defensive holds
* less “infinite counterattack” behavior

## Current Rules

* Movement consumes stamina
* Reactions consume stamina
* Units without enough stamina lose defensive coverage

## Example

Tank:

* Move 0 tiles:

  * 4 reaction attacks possible
* Move maximum range:

  * only 2 reactions remaining

This system encourages:

* holding chokepoints
* fallback lines
* careful advances
* layered formations

---

# Coverage System

## Prepare Stance

Units in “prepare” stance project defensive coverage.

Coverage:

* punishes enemies entering threatened tiles
* uses stamina
* depends on facing direction

## Attack Stance

Attacking usually removes coverage for that turn.

This prevents:

* full offense + full defense simultaneously

## Tank Slow Zones

Tanks additionally create:

* movement suppression
* area denial
* defensive pressure

without requiring direct damage.

# Movement / Facing Rules

## Movement

* Units select movement destination first
* Movement preview tiles shown in cyan

### Dark Cyan Tiles

* maximum movement range
* limited pivot/facing afterwards

## Facing

After moving:

* most units choose facing direction
* coverage depends on facing

### Max-Range Pivot Restriction

Units moving maximum range:

* cannot instantly pivot completely backward
* intended to reward defensive positioning

# Current Design Goals

## Gameplay Goals

* formation tactics
* retreat pressure
* limited reactions
* positional combat
* meaningful terrain
* reduced “one unit solos everything” behavior

## Desired Feel

The game should feel like:

* holding a line
* managing exhaustion
* surviving enemy pressure
* retreating to fallback positions
* maintaining formations under stress

rather than:

* individual units running freely into armies.
